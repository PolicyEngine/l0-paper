\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

The results address the three questions from the introduction: whether informed $L_0$ selection
beats the random, survey-weight, and combinatorial-optimisation baselines at a fixed budget, whether
the retained sample generalizes to held-out targets, and whether the size, geography, and weight
controls behave as intended. All numerical
entries are placeholders pending completed runs.

\subsection{Informed versus uninformed sampling}

Table~\ref{tab:sampling_comparison} compares informed $L_0$ sampling with the three baseline
samplers, random sampling with reweighting, survey-weight sampling, and combinatorial optimisation,
at a matched record budget. The methods share the candidate universe and the target set and differ
only in how records are selected. The comparison reports in-sample and out-of-sample error,
the effective sample size, the spread of fitted weights, and runtime.

The central hypothesis is that informed selection lowers calibration error at a fixed budget,
because the gates retain records the targets actually constrain. The out-of-sample columns test
whether that advantage reflects generalization rather than a tighter fit to the in-sample targets.

\subsection{The size, geography, and weight controls}

A second question is whether the framework gives usable control over the output. Increasing
$\lambda_{L_0}$ should reduce the retained-record count, and increasing $\lambda_{L_2}$ should reduce
the largest fitted weights, without either change destroying the calibration fit.
Figure~\ref{fig:operability} shows those sweeps.
